# Design System Specification: Technical Luminance

## 1. Overview & Creative North Star: "The Synthetic Ledger"
This design system is not a standard dark mode interface; it is a high-precision digital environment designed to feel like a high-end laboratory instrument or a luxury timepiece. The Creative North Star is **"The Synthetic Ledger."** 

We move away from the "web-template" look by embracing a hyper-structured, technical editorial style. This is achieved through:
*   **Intentional Asymmetry:** Aligning large-scale typography to the far left or right while leaving significant negative space to emphasize technical "breathing room."
*   **Luminous Depth:** Utilizing a deep navy foundation (#060e20) contrasted against neon-leaning primary blues to create a "glowing" effect on a matte surface.
*   **Optical Precision:** Every element is governed by a strict geometric relationship, using the Space Grotesk typeface to bridge the gap between mid-century brutalism and futuristic data visualization.

---

## 2. Colors: The Luminary Palette
The color strategy avoids flat, muddy darks in favor of a "backlit" hierarchy. 

### Core Palette
- **Background (`#060e20`):** The absolute foundation. A void-like navy that allows vibrant accents to pop.
- **Primary (`#39b8fd`):** A neon blue designed to act as a light source. Use this for critical interactions.
- **Surface Tiers:**
    - `surface_container_lowest`: `#000000` (Used for deep insets or "well" components).
    - `surface_container_low`: `#091328` (The primary sectioning color).
    - `surface_container_highest`: `#192540` (The most elevated interactive surface).

### The "No-Line" Rule
Traditional 1px solid borders are strictly prohibited for sectioning. To separate content, you must use **Tonal Transitions**. A card should be defined by its shift from `surface` to `surface_container_low`, creating a "molded" look rather than a "boxed" look.

### The "Glass & Gradient" Rule
For primary call-to-actions or high-priority headers, use a subtle linear gradient from `primary` (#39b8fd) to `primary_container` (#1faaef). This adds "soul" and a sense of liquid light. Floating overlays (modals/tooltips) must utilize **Glassmorphism**: a background of `surface_variant` at 60% opacity with a `24px` backdrop-blur.

---

## 3. Typography: Space Grotesk
Space Grotesk’s tabular qualities and geometric quirks are the backbone of this system. It should be treated as a graphical element, not just a way to deliver information.

| Token | Size | Weight | Use Case |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| `display-lg` | 3.5rem | 700 | Hero metrics and editorial impact statements. |
| `headline-md` | 1.75rem | 500 | Section entry points; always leading-aligned. |
| `title-sm` | 1rem | 600 | Card titles and navigational labels. |
| `body-md` | 0.875rem | 400 | Standard data reading and descriptions. |
| `label-sm` | 0.6875rem| 700 (Mono) | Metadata, status tags, and technical specs. |

**Editorial Note:** Use `display-lg` with a tight letter-spacing (-0.02em) to create an authoritative, "technical manual" feel.

---

## 4. Elevation & Depth: Tonal Layering
We reject drop shadows in favor of light-logic.

- **The Layering Principle:** Depth is achieved by stacking. A `surface_container_high` element sitting on a `surface` background creates a natural elevation. 
- **The "Ghost Border" Fallback:** If accessibility requires a container boundary, use the `outline_variant` (#40485d) at **15% opacity**. This creates a "barely-there" suggestion of a border that maintains the system’s clean aesthetic.
- **Ambient Glow:** Instead of black shadows, use a `primary` tinted shadow at 5% opacity for floating buttons to simulate light reflecting off the surface.

---

## 5. Components: Precision Primitives

### Buttons
- **Primary:** Background `primary` (#39b8fd), label `on_primary` (#00324a). Radius: `md` (0.375rem). Use a 2px "inner glow" (white at 10% opacity) on the top edge.
- **Secondary:** Transparent background with a `Ghost Border` using `outline`.
- **Tertiary:** No background. Text uses `primary`.

### Input Fields
- **Container:** `surface_container_low`. 
- **Bottom-Line Detail:** Instead of a full box, use a 2px bottom border in `outline_variant`. When focused, this line transitions to `primary` with a 4px soft outer glow.

### Cards & Lists
- **Prohibition:** Divider lines are banned. 
- **Separation:** Use `surface_container_low` for the card body and `surface_container_high` for the header. Use vertical spacing (minimum 24px) to separate list items.

### Status Chips
- **Success:** `surface_container_high` with a 4px solid circle of `#4ade80` (Vibrant Green).
- **Error:** Background `error_container` (#9f0519) with text `on_error_container`.

---

## 6. Do's and Don'ts

### Do
- **Do** use large amounts of `surface` (#060e20) to make `primary` blue feel like a neon light in the dark.
- **Do** align technical data in a grid that feels like a spreadsheet or a blueprint.
- **Do** use `9999px` (full) rounding for status chips, but keep `md` (0.375rem) for functional components.

### Don't
- **Don't** use pure white (#FFFFFF) for body text. Use `on_surface_variant` (#a3aac4) to reduce eye strain and maintain the "Technical" atmosphere.
- **Don't** use standard shadows. If it needs to float, it needs a backdrop-blur.
- **Don't** use generic icons. Use "Thin" or "Light" weight stroke icons to match the precision of Space Grotesk.

---

## 7. Signature Interaction: The "Pulse"
When a data point updates, the background of that container should briefly transition from `surface_container` to `primary` (at 10% opacity) and fade back. This reinforces the "instrument" nature of the system—it is alive, reactive, and precise.
